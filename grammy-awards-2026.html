2026 Grammy Awards: Kendrick Lamar Dominates With Five Wins

Feb 2, 2026 - Rosé makes history as first K-Pop solo performer

LOS ANGELES — Kendrick Lamar emerged as the biggest winner at the 68th Annual Grammy Awards on Sunday night, taking home five trophies including Album of the Year for "GNX" and Record of the Year for "Not Like Us." The Compton rapper's dominance capped a remarkable year in which he solidified his position as one of the most critically acclaimed artists of his generation while engaging in a highly publicized feud with fellow rapper Drake that captivated the music industry.

The ceremony at Crypto.com Arena also marked a historic moment for K-pop, as BLACKPINK member Rosé became the first Korean female solo artist to perform at the Grammy Awards. The 27-year-old singer delivered a rendition of her hit single "APT." alongside Bruno Mars, drawing widespread praise for her vocal performance and stage presence. The collaboration highlighted the growing global influence of Korean popular music and its increasing acceptance within the American mainstream.

"This is for the culture, for the art form, for hip-hop"

Lamar's victories in the top categories validated his decision to release "GNX" as a surprise album in late 2025, bypassing traditional promotional cycles in favor of letting the music speak for itself. The album, which features collaborations with emerging West Coast artists alongside established stars, received universal acclaim for its lyrical complexity, production innovation, and social commentary.

The Best New Artist award went to British singer-songwriter Olivia Dean, who has gained international recognition for her soulful vocals and introspective songwriting. Dean's debut album "Messy" was also nominated for Best Alternative Music Album, reflecting the Recording Academy's expanded definition of alternative music that now encompasses a broader range of genres and artistic approaches.

In the rock and metal categories, established acts continued to dominate, with veteran bands taking home awards that recognized both recent work and lifetime contributions to the genres. The awards for rock performance and metal performance highlighted the enduring commercial viability of guitar-driven music despite the dominance of hip-hop and pop in overall streaming numbers.

The ceremony featured a visually striking stage design created by New York-based Yellow Studio, which transformed the arena into what designers described as a "golden portal of music." The elaborate production included holographic elements, dynamic lighting, and multiple performance stages that allowed for seamless transitions between award presentations and musical performances.

Latin music continued its ascent in the Grammy ecosystem, with multiple awards recognizing the genre's commercial and artistic achievements. Bad Bunny, who had previously won Album of the Year, presented an award during the ceremony and performed a medley of his recent hits, further cementing his status as a global superstar who transcends language barriers.

The 2026 Grammys also addressed ongoing controversies surrounding the awards process, with Recording Academy CEO Harvey Mason Jr. acknowledging the organization's efforts to diversify its voting membership and expand representation across genres and demographics. The ceremony included tributes to artists who passed away during the previous year, with performances that celebrated their contributions to music history.

Viewership for the broadcast increased 8% from the previous year, with particular growth among younger demographics who streamed the ceremony through various digital platforms. The ratings success provided a boost to the Recording Academy as it continues to navigate the evolving landscape of music consumption and awards relevance in the streaming era.
